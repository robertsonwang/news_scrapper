As Hurricane Matthew barrels toward the Florida coast, residents are bracing for what meteorologists project to be a category 4 storm with winds up to 140 mph. Experts expect Matthew to make landfall Thursday night.

,

Although Florida has a rigorous disaster plan in place and Gov. Rick Scott (R-Fla) has urged some 2 million Floridians to evacuate to South Carolina, storms can be unpredictable, and ensuring you\u2019re prepared for the worst by having sufficient food, medication and hygiene products can help keep your family safe and healthy.

,

If you\u2019re in or near the storm\u2019s predicted path, be sure to take the following steps:

,


Have the following food and water available for use:

,

\xb7 One cooler for drinks and one for food

,

\xb7 Enough water to prove 1 gallon of water per day per person (at least a three-day supply total)

,

\xb7 Other bottled beverages

,

\xb7 Non-perishable food items, such as canned goods (at least a three-day supply)

,

\xb7 \xa0Baby essentials (including food, formula, diapers and bottles)

,


Ensure you have sufficient hygiene and medical supplies:

,

\xb7 Soap

,

\xb7 Feminine hygiene products

,

\xb7 First aid kit

,

\xb7 Antiseptic solution

,

\xb7 Thermometer

,

\xb7 Tweezers

,

\xb7 Bandages

,

\xb7 Children\u2019s, over-the-counter, and prescription medication

,

\xb7 Sunscreen and insect repellent spray

,

\xb7 Adhesive tape
\xa0

,

Don\u2019t forget about pets:

,

\xb7 Have enough water for your pet (enough for three to seven days)

,

\xb7 Dry, non-perishable pet food

,

\xb7 Keep their favorite toy, blanket or pillow and calming aids accessible

,

\xb7 Make sure they have their tags

,

\xb7 For puppies, ensure you have training pads on hand

,

\xb7 Have a collar and/or harness, as well as a non-extendable leash accessible

,

\xb7 Carry a crate, carrier or tank if they use one, and maintain a clean litter box for cats

,

\xb7 Make sure their microchip has up-to-date info

,

\xb7 Keep heartworm medications and any other needed medications on hand

,

\xb7 Ensure their shots are up to date

,


For more ways to make sure your home is prepared for the storm, visit RedCross.org.
