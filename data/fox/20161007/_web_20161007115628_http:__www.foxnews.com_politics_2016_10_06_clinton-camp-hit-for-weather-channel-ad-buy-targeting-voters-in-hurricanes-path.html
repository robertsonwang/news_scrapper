The Hillary Clinton campaign has delayed a planned Florida ad buy on the Weather Channel after facing criticism for targeting voters in the path of Hurricane Matthew.

,

\u201cWe have requested that stations in Florida delay any of those ads on the Weather Channel until after the storm passes,\u201d spokesman Jesse Ferguson said in a statement Thursday.

,

Politico first reported\xa0that the Clinton campaign had planned to spend $63,000 on Weather Channel ads in Florida over a five-day stretch starting Thursday, just as the storm nears the coast.

,

Politico noted that Donald Trump and other candidates have advertised on the same channel this year. But the bid to capture support from anxious Florida residents in the path of a deadly storm that has triggered mass evacuation orders and is expected to strengthen soon into a destructive Category 4 created a bit of an optics problem.

,

\u201cIf they\u2019re out being too political at a time when the country has its prayers with the people affected, I think it could backfire,\u201d Rep. Greg Walden, chairman of the National Republican Congressional Committee, told Fox News earlier Thursday, before the delay was announced.

,

Walden called the ad buy \u201crisky.\u201d

,
